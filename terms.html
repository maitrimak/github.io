Volunteer Terms and Conditions

1. Role and Responsibilities

The volunteer will be responsible for carrying out tasks as outlined by the organization. These tasks may include, but are not limited to, assisting with events, providing administrative support, or engaging in community outreach. The volunteer is expected to perform duties to the best of their ability and adhere to the guidelines set forth by the organization.

2. Compensation

The volunteer position is unpaid. Volunteers will not receive any financial compensation for their time and efforts. However, some expenses such as travel or meal costs may be reimbursed, subject to prior approval by the organization.

3. Training

The volunteer will receive appropriate training for the role to ensure they understand their duties and can perform them effectively. The training will be provided by the organization and may include orientation sessions, manuals, or other learning resources.

4. Support

The volunteer will receive regular support from a designated person within the organization. This may be a volunteer coordinator, supervisor, or other staff member who will provide guidance and assistance as needed. The volunteer is encouraged to reach out for help if they encounter any issues.

5. Insurance

The volunteer will be covered by the organization’s insurance policy during the course of their volunteer work. This coverage includes liability and injury protection while performing volunteer duties. Specific details can be provided upon request.

6. Boundaries

The relationship between the volunteer and the organization is professional and supportive. Volunteers are expected to maintain appropriate boundaries and act in accordance with the organization’s values and code of conduct. Any personal issues or concerns should be communicated respectfully and professionally.

By volunteering with the organization, the volunteer agrees to these terms and conditions. The organization reserves the right to modify or update these terms at any time, with prior notice to the volunteer.
